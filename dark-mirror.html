The Dark Mirror

Original story — no copyright, use it freely

I found it in my nani’s old haveli in Renala Khurd. Tucked behind a broken cupboard. A mirror. Tall, cracked at one corner, frame carved with snakes eating their own tails.

“Don’t look into it after maghrib.”

Nani said it softly. I laughed. Budhiyan hain.

First night, I didn’t see myself. I saw… me. But 5 years older. Taller. Scars on my left cheek I don’t have. He was smiling. I wasn’t.

He raised his hand. I raised mine. But his reflection moved 2 seconds late.

I dropped the mirror. When I picked it up, it was just me again.

Curiosity killed the cat, right?

Second night, 11:11 PM. I stood in front of it again. Older-me was there. This time he held a paper in his hand.

“Don’t go to school tomorrow. Bus 7B will crash.”

Next morning, Bus 7B really crashed. 3 kids died. Older-me saved me.

Third night, he looked tired. He wrote on the glass:

“Trade me one memory. I’ll give you next warning.”

Suddenly I couldn’t remember my 5th birthday. Gone. Deleted.

In exchange, he warned me:

“Don’t trust Ayesha. She will betray you.”

She really did.

One warning = one memory. One answer = one piece of me.

Night 4: He wanted my memory of Ammi’s voice.

Night 5: He wanted the day I learned to ride a cycle.

Night 6: He wanted my name.

By Night 7, I was only 17… but my mind felt 70.

Older-me looked at me with pity. Then wrote:

“Tomorrow, you will break the mirror to save your last memory. Don’t.”

If I break it, I keep what’s left of me.

If I don’t, he takes my face, my memories, my life… and walks out instead.

The mirror cracked more that night. From his side.

At 11:11 PM, his hand touched the glass from inside. My hand touched from outside.

“Which is worse? Dying… or forgetting you ever lived?”

The frame snakes seemed to move.

I had 10 seconds before maghrib ended.

Break the mirror… or let him out.

The mirror waited. Dark. Patient. Hungry.

Moral of “The Dark Mirror”

Some knowledge comes with a price. If you trade your memories, your identity slowly disappears. The past and future are dangerous when obsession becomes stronger than self-worth.
