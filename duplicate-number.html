📞 Duplicate Number

Original Story — Mystery & Supernatural Horror

Chapter 1: The First Call

Nida’s phone buzzed at exactly 2:03 AM.

Unknown number.

The strange thing was that the number was exactly the same as hers.

Half asleep, she answered.

“Close the back window, Nida.”

The voice was hers.

The line went dead.

She laughed, assuming it was a prank.

But when she checked, the back window of her Karachi apartment was wide open.

She was certain she had locked it before going to bed.

Chapter 2: The Warnings

The next night, the phone rang again at 2:03 AM.

Same number.

Same voice.

Only this time it sounded colder.

“Don’t open the third drawer of the almirah.”

Nida froze.

She had planned to clean that exact drawer that day.

For the first time, she felt uneasy.

She ignored the drawer.

The calls stopped for three nights.

Then they returned.

“You’re not listening. Open it.”

Curiosity finally defeated fear.

Inside the drawer, beneath old sweaters, lay a brown leather diary.

She had never seen it before.

Yet when she opened it, her blood ran cold.

The handwriting was hers.

The name was hers.

“Day 1: If you're reading this, I'm already gone.”

Chapter 3: The Diary

Every page described her life perfectly.

Her job at the advertising agency.

Her favorite coffee order.

The scar on her left knee from when she was nine.

Then the entries changed.

They were dated two years into the future.

The final entry was dated tomorrow.

“Tomorrow, 2:03 AM, the call will stop. If you answer, you'll disappear like I did.”

Nida dropped the diary.

Tomorrow was only minutes away.

She unplugged her phone.

She threw the diary into the trash.

And she sat in darkness waiting.

Chapter 4: The Truth

2:03 AM.

The phone lit up.

Unknown number.

Her number.

She refused to answer.

The ringing continued.

The back window rattled violently.

The lights went out.

Then someone stepped out of the almirah.

Another Nida.

Same face.

Same scar.

Holding the diary.

“I wrote the warnings so you'd find the diary.”

“I disappeared because I answered the call.”

“Every time someone reads the diary, they create a new version of me.”

“The call is how we switch places.”

Nida whispered:

“How do I stop it?”

Future Nida handed her the diary.

Then she handed her the phone.

“Don't answer.”

“Burn this.”

“And never buy a second-hand SIM card again.”

A second later, she vanished like smoke.

The phone stopped ringing at 2:04 AM.

Epilogue

Nida burned the diary that same night.

The next day she changed her number.

For six months, everything seemed normal.

Then a friend called her.

“Someone from your old number called me at 2 AM.”

“They knew all my secrets.”

Nida ended the call.

Slowly she checked the back window.

Locked.

But inside the trash bin, beneath the ashes of the diary, rested a fresh page.

The handwriting was unfamiliar.

“Day 1: If you're reading this, I'm already gone.”

Moral

Curiosity can open doors you can't close. Some warnings come too late, and some loops don't end until someone refuses to play their part. Be careful what numbers you pick up... and what stories you start reading.
