The Unsent Messages

Alternate Ending 2: Hopeful Ending

12:01 AM. The message was still typing...

"Please Hamza. Throw it away. Let me rest. Let me finally be free. - You"

I looked at the phone. Then at the door.

10 years of fear. 10 years of warnings. 10 years of living like death was chasing me. Was that really living?

I took a deep breath and walked out.

The river was cold. The air smelled like rain.

I held the Nokia over the water. The screen still glowed.

Message #1006 appeared:

"Don't—"

I pressed delete. All 1005 messages. Gone.

Then I threw the phone.

Splash.

The ripples spread. For a second, I thought I saw the screen light up underwater. One last word:

"Thank you."

Silence.

No new message came. No warning. No buzz.

Just me. Seventeen years old. Standing by a river at midnight with nothing but the sound of water.

I went home and slept without checking my phone. For the first time in years.

Next morning I woke up late. No message. No disaster.

I went to school. Missed the bus. Failed a quiz. Got scolded by Ammi.

Normal things. Human things.

That night, I dreamed of a man. Twenty-seven years old. He looked like me, but tired. At peace.

"You did it. You chose to live, not just survive. Now I can rest."

He turned around and walked into the light. And vanished.

I woke up with tears on my face. But not sad ones.

I don't know what happened to 27-year-old me. Maybe he lived a full life after I threw the phone. Maybe he never existed.

But I do.

And I'm done living for tomorrow's disasters.

From now on, if Bus 7B crashes, I'll help the survivors.

If Amna betrays me, I'll forgive and move on.

If the water tank bursts, I'll build a new room.

Because the bravest thing isn't predicting every fall.

It's getting up every time you fall, without knowing it's coming.

The river still flows. Empty. Quiet. Free.

Just like me.

Moral of Ending

"You can’t outrun tomorrow by reading yesterday’s warnings. Real courage is walking forward, blind, and trusting yourself to handle whatever comes."
