The Missing Roll No. 27

Original story — no copyright, free to use

My name is Hamza. Roll No. 26.
And I was dead.

It started on the first day of 10th grade at Govt High School, Renala Khurd.

“Roll No. 27 - Bilal...?”

Silence.

The teacher frowned.

“Seat 27 will always stay empty.”

Everyone laughed. I laughed too.

But after weeks, curiosity pulled me toward Seat 27. The wood was ice cold.

Inside the desk was an old yellow name tag:

BILAL AHMED - ROLL NO. 27

Written in my handwriting.

That night I dreamed of an exam hall. I was outside. Begging to enter.

Then I saw the date:

April 14, 2025

The day I died in the bus accident.

Next morning:

“Roll No. 26 - Hamza...?”

I answered. But no sound came out.

The teacher marked me absent.

“Roll No. 27 - Bilal...?”

And from the empty desk... I heard my own whisper:

“Present.”

On the blackboard, invisible chalk began writing:

YOU FAILED 9TH. THIS IS YOUR 10TH CHANCE. THE 10TH TIME IS THE LAST TIME.

Every night Seat 27 moved closer to mine. Every night I forgot more about my family.

On the 9th night only two seats remained. Mine. And 27.

EXAM TOMORROW. PASS = LIVE. FAIL = BECOME 27 FOREVER.

At 3 AM I entered the exam hall. Seat 27 waited silently.

Question 1:

“What is your real name?”

I wrote Hamza. The ink vanished.

I wrote Bilal. The ink stayed.

Outside stood two graves. One named Hamza. One empty. Waiting.

Then Seat 27 spoke with my voice:

“Every year one of us lives. The other becomes the empty seat.”

The school was never a school. It was a waiting room for children who died before their final exam.

Roll No. 27 wasn’t a student. It was a curse.

Final Question:

“Who deserves to live?”

My hand shook.

Then... I wrote one word.

The blackboard erased itself.

“Roll No. 26 - Hamza...?”

“Present.”

“Roll No. 27 - Bilal...?”

Silence.

For the first time in 10 years... Seat 27 was warm.

And from the empty desk, I heard a whisper:

“Thank you.”

Moral

“Some seats stay empty not because no one sits there... but because someone chose to stand up for you.”
