❤️ Arranged to Love

Original Story — No Copyright, Free To Use

Ayaan was 26 and lived by code.

If there was a bug, he fixed it. If there was silence, he filled it with keyboard clicks.

His mother decided it was time to "fix" his personal life too.

Noor was 24 and lived by color.

She designed logos by day and avoided arranged meetings by night.

But you can't say no to both parents at once. So she showed up.

The café meeting lasted only 8 minutes.

"Do you code?" he asked.

"Only when Canva crashes," she replied.

Both smiled politely.

Later they texted their siblings the exact same word:

"Pass."

Two weeks later, Noor's younger sister burst into tears.

Her school project website had crashed just 12 hours before submission.

Noor didn't know HTML from her ABCs.

Her mother called Ayaan.

"Beta, you're good with computers, right?"

He was.

So he came over.

He spent the entire night fixing code while Noor made him chai he never asked for.

Between error messages and coffee cups, they talked.

Ayaan said code made sense because it followed rules.

Noor said design mattered because people didn't.

At 4 AM the website finally went live.

At 9 AM her sister received an A+.

After that night, their phones never stopped buzzing.

She sent bad design jokes.

He sent even worse coding puns.

She improved the colors on his portfolio.

He helped showcase her work online where clients could find it.

Neither called it dating.

They called it "Mutual Debugging."

Months passed.

Families started discussing nikah.

Ten minutes before the ceremony, Ayaan quietly pulled Noor aside.

"If you're doing this for your parents, say no now. I won't be angry."

Noor stared at him as if he had missed something obvious.

"Ayaan, you spent a whole night fixing my sister's project when nobody was watching."

"You chose us before there was a 'us'."

"This is just paperwork."

For the first time that day, Ayaan smiled without fear.

Moments later, they both said:

"Yes."

Today they run a small creative agency together.

He builds websites.

She designs brands.

Their office may only have two rooms, but it feels bigger than any dream they once had.

On the wall hangs a golden quote:

"Logic + Heart = Home."

Moral

Love doesn't need a grand confession or perfect timing. Sometimes it's built quietly in favors you didn't have to do, in nights you didn't have to stay, and in choosing someone again and again before you ever say the word "love".

Arranged meetings can lead to chosen hearts.
